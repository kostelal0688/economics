What is the issue?

Over 1.3 million people rely on buses every day to get to school, work, medical appointments, and other important places. Yet, bus service is often slow, delayed, and unreliable. Traffic congestion and a lack of dedicated bus lanes cause buses to move slowly compared to other forms of transportation.

This especially affects low-income communities, seniors, students, and workers who depend on buses as their primary way to travel. When buses are inefficient, it creates inequality because not everyone has access to faster transportation options.

Bus efficiency is not just about speed, it is about fairness, access, and equal opportunity for everyone in the city.

About Us

Mission Statement

The mission of this website is to raise awareness about bus efficiency and fairness. Many people depend on buses every day, and when buses are slow, unreliable, or overcrowded, it impacts school, work, and daily life. We aim to promote a bus system that is reliable, efficient, and accessible to everyone in the community.

Who We Are

We are NYC public high school students working to improve quality of life by raising awareness about bus equity and efficiency. Through this website, we provide resources, statistics, and solutions to highlight how fair and reliable bus service can positively impact students, workers, seniors, and low-income communities who rely on public transportation. Our goal is to inform, inspire action, and support improvements to create a transportation system that works for everyone.
